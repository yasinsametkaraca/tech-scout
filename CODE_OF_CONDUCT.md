# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We — maintainers and contributors — pledge to make participation in this
project a productive and respectful experience for everyone, regardless
of background or experience level. We focus on what helps the project
ship: clear technical communication, honest feedback, and good-faith
collaboration.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour we expect:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Critique ideas, not people. Disagreements are normal; assume the other
  person is acting in good faith and explain your reasoning.
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully and offer it the same way.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, fix them, and move on.
- Keep discussion focused on the work at hand.

Examples of behaviour we will not tolerate:

- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained disruption of discussion.
- Harassment in any form, public or private.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider out of place in a
  professional collaborative setting.

## Scope

This Code applies to all project spaces — issues, pull requests,
discussions, commit messages, and any other public communication that
references the project. It also applies when an individual is officially
representing the project (e.g., speaking at a conference about it,
posting from an official social account).

## Enforcement

Reports go to the maintainer at **`yasinsamet.karaca@gmail.com`** with a
short description of what happened, where, and (if applicable) links to
the relevant content. Reports are kept confidential.

Maintainers will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within **3 business days**.
2. Review the report and gather any necessary additional context.
3. Respond with a decision and any action taken — typically a private
   conversation with the parties involved, a public clarification, an
   edit, or in serious cases, a temporary or permanent ban from project
   spaces.

Maintainers who fail to enforce this Code in good faith may face
consequences chosen by the rest of the maintainer team.

## Attribution

This Code is adapted from common open-source community standards,
notably the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/)
v2.1, and tailored to be short and concrete enough that contributors
will actually read it.
